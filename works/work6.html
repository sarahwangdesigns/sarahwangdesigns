    Process


    I really challenged myself by deciding to try and redesign sunflower seed packaging into something more modern and trendy. I wanted sunflower seeds to be more recognized as a healthy food, especially a great alternative to people with nut allergies. I specifically targeted a younger and more health conscious audience in this repackaging and branding choice.



    Logo


    I chose to create a simple, clean, and modern logo containing a simple sunflower seed and a golden yellow circle to indicate the disk of a sunflower and/or a sun. I chose the font Memphis because it was as simple, easy, clean, yet a little rustic to match the idea of sunflower seeds, but in a modern way.


    Biomimicry


    I choose sunflowers, sunflower seeds, and honeycomb to base the biomimicry of my packaging off of.


    Prototype 1


    My first prototype was based on the idea of recreating the look of a sunflower with how my packaging unfolded. I made a hexagonal tray to hold any sunflower seed shells as the base of the packaging and sunflower seed shaped to-go/portion controlled/snackable packages of sunflower seeds as tear-away foldable petals.


    Feedback


    My peers thought the idea was really creative and unique, however there was a problem of how it would sit on the shelf in stores and how that would work. Wrapping it in a saran wrap or plastic was an option or making a cap for it, however it still did not sit or display well on shelfs in grocery stores.


    Final Product


    I brainstormed another design that could be a reusable container that holds the sunflower seed packets. The container would be hexagonal and tall and wide enough to hold 14 packets of to-go sunflower seed packets if organized in a fibonnacci swirl pattern comfortably. The lid of the containers and packets of sunflower seeds would be color coded to indicate different flavors.
